My Style

• My style is to create game plans. The first thing I do when I find out that I have to make an important decision is come up with a game plan. I think of multiple angles to approach the situation. When it comes to hosting a social gathering of any sort, I come up with a game plan to make it the least bit stressful and to use my resources to their fullest.

What I Value

• I value relationships. My very best friend, Dallin Tavenner, once told me that the most important thing in our lives are our relationships. I agree with him 100 percent. We both love talking about the gospel, and it’s the gospel of Jesus Christ that has helped us come to that realization. One relationship that I especially value is my relationship with my mother. She is very precious to me, because she taught me the values that I have today.

• Another thing that I value is the Gospel of Jesus Christ. I’ve learned so much from it every day, and everything I learn from it adds value to my life.

What I Don’t Have Patience For

• I don’t have patience for people that purposefully try to make a fool out of others. Whether a person is trying to make me feel like a fool or if they’re trying to make someone else feel like a fool, that boils my blood. People that do this aren’t healthy to be around, and I never enjoy being near them.
• Another thing that I don’t have patience for is people that belittle others. No one is more important than anyone else. By living the Gospel of Jesus Christ, I’ve come to learn and believe that none of us are better than any of our brothers or sisters.

The Best Way to Communicate with Me

• My most preferred method of communication is face to face. I feel like that is the most genuine way to communicate. Body language is huge for me. It’s hard for me to completely understand a person’s intentions over text or phone call. If I had to rate my most preferred methods of communication from best to worst, then it would be: 1- Face-to-Face, 2- Video Call, 3- Phone Call, 4- Text, 5- Email.

How to Help Me

• I tend to let people know when I am struggling. I wear my heart on my sleeve. You will know when I need help. Whether it be emotional support or technical support, you will know. The best way to go about it is to calm me down, affirm that everything will be alright, and to help me come up with a game plan.

What People Misunderstand About Me

• What people misunderstand about me is that I don’t want to spend time with them. I love spending time with friends, acquaintances, classmates, and co-workers. I sometimes don’t show the desire to be around others too well, because I’m afraid of rejection. Completely excluding romantic rejection, plutonic rejection still hurts. The fear of missing out stems from that.
